In this project, we use it to calculate the growth rate ($k$) reliably, even when noise is present in the lab data.Logarithmic Transformation: Cell growth is an exponential process. To transform it into a linear process, we apply a logarithmic transformation to the concentration (base 2):$$ \\ \log\_2(N) = \log\_2(N\_0) + k \cdot t
$$$$$$Where $\log_2(N)$ is the vertical axis, and $t$ is the horizontal axis.Finding the Slope: After the transformation, the data points form a straight line, and SciPy's linear regression finds the slope of this line:$$ \\ \text{Slope} = k
$$$$$$The slope ($k$) is the specific growth rate per unit of time, representing the number of doublings (Generations) per unit of time.$R^2$ Metric: We also calculate the Coefficient of Determination ($R^2$). This value (between 0 and 1) shows how well the straight line fits the $\log_2$ data. An $R^2$ closer to 1.0 indicates a better fit for the exponential growth phase.âš ï¸ Important Usage Note:This mathematical model is valid only for the exponential growth phase of the culture.
